My approach

The bar was triangulated from three sources, then thirty candidates were run through a two-stage filter — hard requirements first, fit second.

→ STEP 01

Read the source material

Live JD, three Slack updates from the hiring team, and the five profiles already hired. The hires reveal what Paraform's team actually weights, beyond what the JD makes explicit.

Define the bar using Paraform's framing

Product · Engineering · Business — the intersection Paraform names at the close of the JD.

→ STEP 03

Funnel, not rank

Hard filter on the Avoid list. Soft filter on JD evidence visible in role descriptions. Thirty candidates resolve to eleven actions.

→ STEP 04

Validate against hires

If the framework holds, hires #26, #28, #30 should map onto the archetypes. They do — and #27 reveals where the AVOID filter overreaches.

02 / The Bar

Paraform's JD names the bar: "the intersection of product, engineering, and business."

The JD closes with this line. The framework below uses Paraform's framing directly.

From the JD: "Your work will directly shape how companies hire… if you want to operate at the intersection of product, engineering, and business — this is the job for you." The yellow center represents the trait Paraform names last in the JD, because it binds the other three: high ownership.

Product-minded

06 / Next Actions

What to do this week, candidate by candidate.

Three principles shape every move. Eleven per-candidate actions, ranked by urgency, follow.

→ PRINCIPLE
Bring a calibrated take, not a list

The recruiter is the only person reading the whole pipeline. Depth beats volume. Every push needs a one-line "why now" the HM can repeat.

→ PRINCIPLE
Catch what only the recruiter sees

Stage-drift bias is invisible to the HM from one resume at a time. Pulling the brake on #19, verifying the bar on #25 before offer signs, and advocating override on #22 and #9 are the moves only the recruiter can make.

→ PRINCIPLE
Protect HM attention like it's the recruiter's own

Cancel a screen when the data is clear. Pause an onsite when the archetype is wrong. Re-route when the role is wrong. Each of the eleven actions below applies one of these moves.
